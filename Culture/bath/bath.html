PUBLIC BATH 1752 HISTORY

Mauban's Public Bath was built in 1725 by Dumagats, an indigenous tribe from the group of Negritos. The Dumagats built the bath from limestone and seashells, with a 200-meter canal that channels water from a stream. Water is collected in a large tank and flows continuously through the tank's many faucets. Back then, the Public Bath served as a communal venue for bathing, washing, and drinking water, subsequently aiding in the first civilization of Mauban. Today, the canal is already blocked, and water no longer reaches the bath. The Public Bath now serves as an artifact, declared as an important cultural property by the National Museum of the Philippines.
